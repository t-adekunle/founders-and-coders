Photography

There are many different kinds of photography, here I will discuss the different genres of photography and some of my favourite photographers.

Genres of Photography

There are a lot of different genres of photography to explore. Some of my favourite types include:

  • Portraiture
  • Fashion
  • Landscape
  • Photojournalism

Portraiture

Portrait photography involves photographing a person, usually with a focus on their face. There can be creative portraits with lots of dramatic shadows and there can be simpler portraits that are more appropriate for corporate headshots and identification images. Annie Leibovitz and Dave LaChapelle are two of examples of portrait photographers whose work I really enjoy.

Fashion

Fashion photography is an incredibly creative form of photography where you can see a merging of commercial and fine art styles. There is as much focus on the creative vision and narrative as there is on the clothes.

Nick Knight is a great example of the breadth of creativity in fashion photography and is one of my favourite photographers.

Landscape

Landscape photography focuses on the outdoors. The photographer captures the feeling and the mood of the setting that they envisioned in the outdoor environment. The main purpose of landscape photography is to capture the beauty of nature. Landscape photography can take the form of abstract images - like Frances Seward or capture a more traditional view of the landscape. The work of Ansel Adams is a good example of this.

Photojournalism

Photojournalism makes use of visual storytelling with the purpose of communicating a news story through images. Some people connect to images more than they do to words and so photo journalism can be very useful in educating people about global issues. For instance some of Dorothea Lange's work helped raise awareness of struggling farmers during the depressions era.
